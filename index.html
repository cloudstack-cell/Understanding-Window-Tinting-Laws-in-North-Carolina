North Carolina Window Tinting Regulations

North Carolina law specifies the Visible Light Transmission (VLT) percentage, which is the amount of light that must pass through a tinted window. Here‚Äôs what you need to know:

1. Windshield Tinting

  • Tint is only allowed on the top 5 inches of the windshield.
  • The tint must have a VLT of 35% or higher and must not reflect more than 20% of light.

2. Front Side Windows

  • The tint must have a minimum VLT of 35%, meaning it should allow at least 35% of light to pass through.

3. Rear Side Windows

  • For sedans, the VLT must also be at least 35%.
  • For SUVs and vans, any level of tint is permitted.

4. Rear Windshield

  • Similar to rear side windows, sedans require a VLT of 35%, while SUVs and vans have no specific limit.

5. Reflectivity Limit

  • North Carolina limits window reflectivity to 20%, ensuring that tint does not cause glare for other drivers.

Medical Exemptions for Window Tinting

North Carolina allows medical exemptions for individuals with conditions that require additional protection from sunlight. To qualify, drivers must:

  1. Obtain a written prescription from a licensed physician.
  2. Apply for a medical exemption permit through the North Carolina Division of Motor Vehicles (DMV)..

Once approved, drivers can install darker tints than the standard legal limits.
